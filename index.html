Big Picture

Structure

  • Frequency: 6 training days / week (Sun off)
  • Microcycle layout: Mon Lower Strength → Tue Speed/Acro → Wed Upper Strength → Thu Intensive Plyo/Max‑V → Fri Total‑Body Power → Sat Forms/Tempo
  • Deloads: End of Weeks 4, 8, 12; unload after Week 16

Mesocycles

  1. Sept (W1–4) Accumulation: tissue tolerance, extensive plyos, accel speed
  2. Oct (W5–8) Intensification: heavier strength, intensive plyos, add max‑V
  3. Nov (W9–12) Conversion: power emphasis, reactive jumps, complex skills
  4. Dec (W13–16) Peak & Maintain: sharpness, low junk volume

Guidelines

  • Land quiet; keep knees/hips stacked; full recoveries on speed
  • Use RPE 7–8 most days; hit RPE 9 only in Oct Week 7
  • Track CMJ, 30 m, single‑leg bounds, and key kicks (tornado/540)

Daily Sessions — with Stretching for Every Block

MON — Lower Strength + Intro Plyo

  1. Warm‑up (10–12′): rope 2′, dynamic leg swings, ankle rocks, hip CARs, 2×10 m A‑march → A‑skip
  2. Plyo (extensive/rhythm): pogo hops 3×30; lateral line hops 3×20/side; low‑hurdle march‑to‑hop 3×10 m
  3. Strength (Lower):
    • Front Squat 5×5 → 5×3 (see progression)
    • Trap‑bar DL 4×5 (alt. RDL 4×6)
    • Front‑foot‑elevated split‑squat 3×8/side
    • Nordic hamstring 3×5 (assist as needed)
    • Standing calf raise 4×10 + tibialis raise 3×15
  4. Wushu‑specific: Horse → Bow stance transitions 4×20 m (snap punch on hits); sweep → pop‑up to stance 4×4/side
  5. Stretching (post 8–12′):
    • Couch stretch 2×45 s/side
    • PNF hamstring (strap) 2×30 s/side
    • 90/90 hip flow 2×60 s + Frog stretch 2×45 s
    • Knee‑to‑wall dorsiflexion 3×10/side; calf wall hold 2×45 s
    • T‑spine open books 2×8/side; wrist flexor/extensor holds 2×30 s

TUE — Speed (Acceleration) + Wushu Jumps/Acro

  1. Warm‑up: buildups 3×20 m; ankling → A‑skip; wall‑drives
  2. Speed (accel): 6–10×20 m starts or sled pulls (10–20% BW); walk‑back recovery
  3. Plyo (vertical power): CMJ 4×4; box jump 4×3 (soft land)
  4. Wushu jumps/acro: tornado, inside→jump‑inside, aerial, B‑twist progressions (see month section)
  5. Stretching (post):
    • Sciatic nerve glide 2×10/side
    • Couch stretch 2×45 s; psoas release (ball) 60–90 s/side
    • Standing toe‑reach with long exhale 2×30 s
    • Side‑lying windmills 2×8/side; wrist series 2×30 s

WED — Upper Strength + Tech/Mobility

  1. Warm‑up: scap CARs, band pull‑aparts, wrist prep
  2. Strength (Upper):
    • Push Press 5×3 → 6×2 (see progression)
    • Weighted Pull‑up 4×5
    • Ring row 3×10; Dip 3×8 (or DB floor press 4×8)
    • Core: Pallof press 3×12/side; Hollow rocks 3×20″
  3. Wushu‑specific tech: Nanquan power clusters 6×(5–8 s) @ 90–120″ rest; staff fast chains 4×20 m
  4. Stretching (post):
    • Lat door stretch 2×45 s; shoulder extension w/ stick 3×20 s
    • Pec doorway stretch 2×45 s
    • Wrist flexor/extensor 2×30 s each
    • Thoracic bridge prep holds 3×20–30 s

THU — Plyometrics (Intensive) + Speed (Max‑V)

  1. Warm‑up: wicket run‑ups 2×20 m; ankling → dribbles
  2. Plyo (intensive/reactive): hurdle hops 4–5×4; drop→stick 3×3; depth jumps (progress height); single‑leg bounds 4×20 m
  3. Speed (max‑V): flying 20–30s (e.g., 20 m build + 20–30 m fly) × 4–6; 3–4′ rest
  4. Stretching (post):
    • Calf/Achilles long holds 2×60 s (step or slant)
    • Tibialis raises 2×20; big‑toe extension 2×30 s
    • Banded hip distraction 2×45 s/side; adductor rockbacks 2×10
    • Windmills 2×8; gentle hip internal‑rotation holds 2×30 s

FRI — Total‑Body Power/Strength + Wushu Power

  1. Warm‑up: med‑ball OH, scoop, rotational throws 3×3 each
  2. Strength/Power:
    • Power Clean (or High‑pull) 5×3 (later clusters)
    • Back Squat 4×4 (or 5×3 depending on month)
    • Hip Thrust 3×6–8
    • Seated box jump (high) 4×2
  3. Wushu‑specific power: 6–8× finishers (4–6 s): explosive punches, stomps, jump‑in combos; full intent
  4. Stretching (post):
    • Couch 2×45 s; PNF hamstring 2×30 s/side
    • Glute stretch (figure‑4) 2×45 s/side
    • Ankle dorsiflexion (weighted) 3×10
    • T‑spine open books 2×8/side; wrist series 2×30 s

SAT — Forms/Combos + Tempo + Long Mobility

  1. Wushu block: Sections at 80–95% quality, 6–10 sets of 15–25 s; competition sequences; weapons speed ladders 6×15 s
  2. Tempo conditioning: 6–10×100 m @ ~70% with 60–75″ rest or 6–8×200 m easy tempo (HR ≤ 150–160)
  3. Long Mobility (20–25′):
    • Front split progression 3×45 s/side; side split sliders 3×30 s
    • Bridge prep (thoracic) holds 3×20–30 s
    • Shoulder extension w/ stick 3×20 s; lat door stretch 2×45 s
    • Weighted dorsiflexion 3×10; wrist pulses 2×20 + back‑of‑hand 2×10

Progressive Overload — Month by Month

September (Weeks 1–4) — Accumulation
  • Strength: build volume
    • Front Squat: W1 5×5@70%, W2 5×5@72.5%, W3 5×5@75%, W4 deload 3×5@60%
    • Trap‑bar DL: W1–3 4×5@70–77.5%, W4 3×5@60%
    • Upper: Push Press 5×3@70–75%; Pull‑up 4×5; W4: −30% volume
  • Plyos: extensive bias; 100–120 contacts/wk → W4: 70–80; no depth‑jump rebounds
  • Speed: accel focus 150–220 m; sled 10–15% BW
  • Wushu skills: tornado (chest→chin markers), inside→jump‑inside; aerial preps (cartwheel‑no‑hands)
October (Weeks 5–8) — Intensification
  • Strength: more intensity, fewer reps
    • Front Squat: W5 5×4@80%, W6 6×3@82.5%, W7 5×3@85%, W8 deload 3×4@65%
    • Back Squat (Fri) 4×4@80–85%
    • RDL 4×6@RPE8; Nordic 4×4
    • Push Press W5–7 6×2@80–85% (bar speed ≥ 0.6–0.8 m/s if tracked)
  • Plyos: introduce intensive elements; 120–150 contacts/wk; depth jumps @ 20–50 cm rising W5→W7; W8 low
  • Speed: keep accel; add max‑V: flying 20s (4–5) → 30s (4–6)
  • Wushu skills: intro 540 on mat; B‑twist half turns; nanquan clusters 8–10×6 s
November (Weeks 9–12) — Conversion to Power
  • Strength: power focus (move 65–75% fast)
    • Front Squat 6×2@65–70% (dynamic)
    • Power Clean clusters 4×(2+2)@70–75% (20–30″ intra‑set)
    • Trap‑bar jump squat 5×3@20–30% BW
    • Push Jerk 6×2@75–80%
  • Plyos: highest intensity; moderate volume; depth jumps 50–60 cm 4×3; rebound hurdles 4–6; single‑leg drop→bound 3×3/side
  • Speed: emphasize max‑V; limited assisted flys (3–4 reps only)
  • Wushu skills: 540 solid → combo; B‑twist full w/ mat; forms 90–95% w/ full recovery
  • W12 deload: cut volumes ~40%; remove most intensive depth jumps
December (Weeks 13–16) — Peak & Maintain
  • Strength: neural maintenance, low fatigue — Front Squat 4×2@75–80% (W13–15), W16 3×2@70%; Power Clean 4×2@70%; TB jump squat 3×2@20–25%
  • Plyos: 70–90 contacts/wk; depth jumps 30–40 cm 3×3; hurdle‑hop waves 3×4
  • Speed: 4–6× flying 20–30s; 200–260 m total; long rests
  • Wushu skills: polish sequences; 6–10×10–18 s at 95–100%; quality jump‑spins
  • W16: re‑test CMJ/30 m/skills; unload

Wushu‑Specific Exercise Progressions

Tornado Kick

  • Sep: height markers chest→chin; 6×4/side
  • Oct: add quarter‑turn exit; 5×3/side
  • Nov: link to stomp/strike; 5×2/side
  • Dec: competition‑tempo combos (2–3 elements) × 6–8

540 Kick

  • Oct: intro to mat, 5×2/side
  • Nov: land & stick, link 540→stomp/sweep 5×2
  • Dec: quality singles in combos, 6–8 total reps

Aerial / B‑Twist

  • Sep: cartwheel‑no‑hands 5×3; snap‑down drills
  • Oct: Aerial → lunge stick 5×2/side; B‑twist half‑turn 6×2
  • Nov: Aerial → split‑step; B‑twist full w/ mat 5×2
  • Dec: best version only, 4–6 total reps

Nanquan Power Clusters

6–10×6–8 s, full intent, 90–120″ rest. Focus: stomp‑punch, horse→bow reactivity, breath timing (fa‑li).

Weapons Speed Ladders

Staff/sabre 6×15 s bursts; goal = smooth chain speed, crisp stops; rest ≥90″.

Testing & Auto‑Regulation

Test Weeks

Test before Tuesday session on Week 1, Week 8, and Week 16:

  • Countermovement Jump (best of 3)
  • 30 m sprint (10 m split if possible)
  • Single‑leg hop (5 bounds distance)
  • Key skills: tornado and 540 kick height/consistency

Auto‑Regulation Keys

  • Bar speed slows drastically → cut a set or lower load
  • Landings get noisy/valgus → reduce height/contacts
  • Target RPE 7–8; avoid accumulating junk fatigue

Safety & Recovery Anchors

Technique

  • Quiet landings; stack hip‑knee‑ankle; use soft surfaces for intensive plyos
  • 48–72 h between intensive plyo exposures

Nutrition

  • Protein ≥ 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day; carbs around speed/plyo days
  • Creatine 3–5 g/day if already tolerated

Care

  • Foam roll calves/peroneals; add tibialis raises Mon/Thu
  • Wrist/shoulder prep every session for weapons/acro
  • Sleep 8 h, hydrate, nasal breathing on cooldowns
